From the beautiful imagery to the garden of Eden allegory, to the compassion shown to each character, this movie was transcendent. The men who hate this movie hate women. This movie takes pains to explain that it's "not all men" (see Alan), but that patriarchy harms everyone, men (or "Ken") included.

This film delivers on both childlike joy and complex human emotions, particularly those of women and girls. The whole theater was packed when I saw it, mostly with women donning pink. It was a heartwarming experience.

* SPOILERS BELOW * The most substantial criticism I've seen is that the subplot with the Mattel executives is not neatly wrapped up. However, this movie is very metatextual, and I think it would be betraying its own message that Barbie, the doll or the brand, did not and cannot "solve feminism." I think the digs at Mattel could have real-world benefits, and Greta Gerwig incredible for getting even this much satire through.

Is it very female centric? Yes. Might it make men uncomfortable? Yes. Is that necessarily a bad thing? I don't think so.

While I do find myself feeling bad for the Ken's, they are being used as an allegory for how women are so often treated (ie. Ken only exists if Barbie looks at him, not being treated as an equal, the dig at the end that eventually they will have as much power as women in the real world). It's a very blatant girl power movie, so I don't expect men to love it. I just hope that, maybe, it makes them think.

There's a couple things I don't love about it, which is why it's only 9 stars. It didn't need to tie up into a perfect little bow at the end, but I understand why it was written that way. The teenager character was meh for me. I feel like she was just kind of thrown in so we could have the Mom's story, but she wasn't terrible.

But overall, it was entertaining, pretty, and Alan is fantastic!
